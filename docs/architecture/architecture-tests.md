# Mission Architecture Tests

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Draft

Mission Architecture Tests are reflective checks used to evaluate whether an architecture remains purposeful, understandable, governable and connected to reality. They are not a compliance score.

1. **Mission Test** — Can the existence of this element be justified by a Mission?
2. **Objective Test** — Which Objective depends on it?
3. **Capability Test** — Which Capability does it provide or enable?
4. **Reality Test** — Can it be traced to a real-world activity, need, obligation or outcome?
5. **Ownership Test** — Who owns, operates and may change it?
6. **Evidence Test** — How do we know it creates value or performs as intended?
7. **Explainability Test** — Can its purpose and behaviour be explained clearly?
8. **Replaceability Test** — Can it be replaced independently, or is coupling justified?
9. **Failure Test** — Which Missions, Objectives and Actors are affected if it fails?
10. **AI Test** — Can an AI determine why it exists, what evidence supports it and where human authority remains?
11. **Human Test** — Would an operator or accountable owner understand why it matters?
12. **Future Test** — Would the architecture still make conceptual sense if the current technology were replaced?

## Design Readiness Review

Before implementation begins, verify that a Mission is defined; Objectives and Capabilities are explicit; Actors, ownership, Assets and Resources are understood; relevant Policies are identified; Reality Traceability is documented; success and evidence are defined; and AI responsibilities are separated from human accountability.

> The purpose of architecture is not to produce diagrams. The purpose of architecture is to improve reality.
