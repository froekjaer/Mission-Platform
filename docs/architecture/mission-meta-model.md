# Mission Meta Model

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Foundation Draft

## Purpose

The Mission Reference Model answers **What exists?** The Mission Meta Model answers **How may those things relate?**

It defines relationship semantics, identity, ownership, time, state, behaviour and validation rules.

## Primary relationship chain

```text
Reality Anchor ⇅ Mission ⇅ Objective ⇅ Capability ⇅ Service ⇅ Workflow or Component ⇅ Infrastructure and Resources ⇅ Evidence
```

The chain illustrates a common traceability path rather than a mandatory hierarchy.

## Relationship properties

Every significant relationship should be capable of expressing source, target, relationship type, inverse meaning, owner, validity period, status, supporting evidence, rationale and provenance.

## Ownership and accountability

Important elements and relationships shall identify accountable ownership. Ownership may include roles such as accountable, responsible, consulted, informed, operator, custodian or authority.

## Identity and time

Persistent elements require stable identity independent of name, implementation and location. The model shall distinguish when something is true in reality, when it was recorded, when it became effective and when it ceased to be valid.

## State and behaviour

Events may change state. Workflows may respond to events. Decisions may authorise transitions. Policies may constrain allowed transitions.

## Validation questions

- Does every Objective contribute to a Mission?
- Does every Capability support an Objective or another justified purpose?
- Does every important element have an owner?
- Can operational evidence be traced to the mission it may affect?
- Are decisions and exceptions documented?
- Are relationships explainable in both directions?

## AI reasoning

AI may navigate relationships, detect gaps, generate explanations and propose improvements. AI-generated conclusions shall retain provenance and shall not replace human accountability.
