# The Reality Principle

> Architecture must never lose its connection to reality.

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Foundation Draft

## Origin and attribution

During the early development of Mission Framework, **Morten Thrane** introduced the expression:

> **“Remtræk til Virkeligheden.”**

A belt drive transfers force between moving parts. If the belt breaks, the connection is lost. The same is true of architecture: when architecture loses its connection to the operational reality it was created to support, it becomes an abstract model with diminishing practical value.

The English architectural term used in Mission Framework is **Reality Traceability**. The original Danish expression is deliberately preserved because of its technical precision and memorable engineering intuition.

Credit for the expression **“Remtræk til Virkeligheden”** belongs to **Morten Thrane**.

## The principle

Every architectural element shall maintain an explicit, explainable, verifiable and bidirectional relationship to the reality it represents.

Reality is both the origin and the destination of architecture.

## More than traceability

The Reality Principle reaches upward to purpose, values, societal outcomes and human needs; downward to decisions, services, components, infrastructure, configuration and operational details; and back through telemetry, observations, outcomes and evidence.

There shall be no architectural blind ends.

Every element should answer:

1. Where do I come from?
2. What real-world purpose do I serve?
3. Who receives value from me?
4. How do we know I continue to work?

## The continuous loop

```text
Reality ⇅ Mission ⇅ Objectives ⇅ Capabilities ⇅ Services ⇅ Components ⇅ Infrastructure ⇅ Operations ⇅ Evidence ⇅ Reality
```

Every relationship should be bidirectional, explainable and open to verification.

## Reality anchors

A **Reality Anchor** is evidence that connects an architectural element to an observable part of the real world, such as a human need, legal obligation, physical asset, operational activity, measurable outcome, incident, sensor observation or societal effect.

## Final reflection

Technology, organisations and architectures will evolve. Reality remains the ultimate point of reference.

As expressed by Morten Thrane:

> **“Remtræk til Virkeligheden.”**
